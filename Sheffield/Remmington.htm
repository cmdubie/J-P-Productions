Welcome Brave of Heart!

This attraction will take you back in time to a location of an unsolved murder, and if you have the guile and fortitude, you can try and solve the mystery and win a prize!

The "Remmington Mansion Murders" will take you on a journey through the dark and creepy Remmington Mansion, filled with apparitions, ghosts, and a heinous plot. Discover the sinister history of the house, by finding clues and facts along the way.

This is not a "spook house" with annoying Halloween decorations. This is a well thought out haunted attraction, that has been carefully planned to frighten you, as well as lead you through the many twists and turns of a complicated murder mystery story. If you delve deep enough, you will discover a horrible nightmare that will haunt you days after you leave!

We hope you enjoy your stay at the Remmington Mansion. Don't get too scared! And good luck solving the mystery!


Helpful Hints

To obtain optimum satisfaction from this adventure, please execute the following hints:

1) Turn your day setting to Midnight! A haunted house is far more atmospheric at night!

2) Turn off the Sim music.There is subtle music and sound cues at the location that can not be heard if you have your media on. So turn up your volume and turn off the music!

3) Many areas will require you to use Mouselook. (View/Mouselook) This will help you navigate in tight spots. We highly recommend Mouselook for the whole adventure. And don't cheat by camming through walls! Take one room at a time!

4) Click on everything you see in the Mansion! Click on items to receive note cards. Click on objects to interact with them as well. (You can sit on chairs, lay on beds, open doors, etc...) Some events are "triggered" when you get close enough to them, so stay there a bit and wait for the scene to "rez." Some objects will give you a sound too. Pretty creepy stuff.

5) Read ALL note cards that are offered to you. When they pop up on your screen, read them from start to finish. It is there the mystery will unravel. You will find valuable clues, a few "MacGuffins," and a few "Red Herrings" too!

6) You may want to stay in each defined area for awhile. The reason for this is some things will rez slower than others. You may miss some cool effects or a subtle clue because Second Life's lag is taking its good old time rezzing something here. Give it a minute and watch the room closely.

7) When you are done investigating the house, return here. You will see a "Mystery Answer Board" sitting here. Leave it alone for now - do not click it or your mystery will be over! (Sorry, the sign can not take back your "click!") After you have explored the house and solved the crime, come back to the board and select your answer. You will see 6 potential perpetrator's names on the board. Select one and only one! If you are right, you will receive a prize box! If you chose poorly, you will receive nothing and can not select again. So think carefully about your answer. If you are with a group, one person should answer only. Everyone should share their facts, and one "detective" click the box. The prize can be shared by the winner anyway, it will be transferable.

8) Pay attention to the details. This attraction has been carefully created with many details that include rich textures, hidden clues, and mini-scenes with plot offering vignettes. Don't just "romp" through the house. There is no particular order to solve the mystery, but the more clues you gather, the easier it will be to solve the crime.

9) Please tell your friends and contacts about this place! Invite them to solve the mystery with you! It is actually a great night of fun and intrigue for a group of friends! Who will solve the mystery first?

10) Please tip the tip box near the front sign if you enjoyed yourself! We have to pay tier here and your help will keep this attraction open! Any donations are appreciated!

We hope you enjoy your journey at the Remmington Mansion. Don't get too frightened! And good luck solving the mystery!

On that note, let us start your evening off with this...

"It was a hot and dark summer night in Boston, Massachusetts on that fateful night in 1820. The Remmington family had just gathered in the living room for some reading and relaxation. Mr. Remmington , smoking his pipe, watched his two children playing with their wooden toys on the floor. Mrs. Remmington, laying on the divan, was entering notes into her beloved diary..."
