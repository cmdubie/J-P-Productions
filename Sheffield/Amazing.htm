"A-Maze-ing Adventures" is owned by Zoltan Mint, game designer and Game Master of many role playing games on Second Life.

This product line includes passageways, rooms, dungeon dressings, traps, and other modular pieces to help you build adventure structures for your virtual role-play experiences in Second Life. These modular stone labyrinths have been created to allow you to easily build everything from castles, dungeons and crypts. They are all low prim and very affordable!

Corridors

This line of A-Maze-ing Adventure Modular Stonework Labyrinths include: long, medium, and short passageways. You will find corridors that have both tall and low ceilings, as well as hallways that have various types of openings in their middle or on their ends. This makes building complex dungeons easy, for all you have to do is line up room doors to the corridor's openings!


About the A-Maze-ing Adventures Modular Stonework Labyrinth Pieces

Each particular structure can be used by itself or added to the other A-Maze-ing Adventure modular pieces, to construct anything from small structures, to huge elaborate dungeons!

Feel free to use your own real life maps (or even commercial adventure maps), to construct the dungeons and labyrinths of your design or choice in Second Life. Each piece can be moved and aligned to fit to each other. (You can align a corridor to a room, etc.. to create a large encounter area of your choice.)

Rezzing Instructions

After you rez the gift box, (you must have permission to rez in the land you are in), right click on the box, (it looks like a small brick pillar), and select "Open." Then select "Copy to Inventory," and the contents will copy to your inventory, under a new folder called "A-Maze-ing Adventures Delivery Box."

Find the new item just copied in your inventory and drag them out onto your land. Be careful, some items are very big, as in the case of the rooms entitled "Big." The item will appear in-world and can be moved by you into the desired position. (A copy will remain in your inventory, which is good, in case you damage the one rezzed.)

Note: A few items we sell consist of multiple pieces. (Like in the case of rooms or corridors that have stairs.) It is best to get into "edit mode" when you drag these items out. This allows you to move the all the pieces together into the position you desire. Modules that contain multiple items have a different symbol in your inventory, (not a single box icon), but looking like multiple boxes stacked on each other.

To move modular pieces - Right click the piece and select edit. In edit mode, you can move the room or corridor by selecting the red, blue or green arrow handles you will see while in this mode. Slowly move the object to the location you want. In edit mode, you can also rotate each piece by checking the Rotate box. Line up rooms and corridors in any manner you wish, creating all types of configurations.

Remember, each piece, (except a few items we could not make with permissions), are full copy and modifiable. This allows you to rez many rooms or corridors to create the exact labyrinth you need. You can alter the size and shape of each module as well, but be careful to leave back up copies of the original in your inventory. A-Maze-ing Adventures will not be responsible or replace any lost or damaged pieces that you edit and manipulate.

Hints:

- Try rezzing out multiple copies of rooms and corridors and rotating them into new layout designs. Doors and archways can be removed if desired and room dimensions can be adjusted to fit what ever lay out you need.

- Make sure you look for all the other modular pieces that we have available. We have almost every shape and size dungeon room and corridor you can imagine. Buy them all to create huge labyrinths and buildings.

- To build dungeons and labyrinths in the sky, (perhaps you do not have enough ground space), we recommend rezzing a sky platform in the sky where you wish first. Then build the dungeon on this platform, and when finished, delete the sky platform. Sky platforms are easily made or found for free on Marketplace.

- If a piece does not have enough doors or archways to fit the layout you are trying to reproduce, we recommend using "false door" copies to represent the openings. If a player decides to use the door, they can easily TP to the other side during game play.

- Stack multiple rooms/corridors on top of each other to get the multiple level effect. Steps can be used to connect these levels.

- You are not limited to just dungeons or labyrinths with A-Maze-ing Adventure modules. You can build wonderful castles, fortresses, and even living locations with these pieces too!

Thank you again and we hope you enjoy your labyrinth pieces! If you have any questions or problems, please feel free to contact Zoltan Mint!
