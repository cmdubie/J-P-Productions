The Sheffield Roxy Theater

The Sheffield Roxy Theater is a full functioning entertainment center that houses the Roxy Stage. This building was constructed to bring live theatrical productions, vaudeville shows, and burlesque to Second Life audiences. The house will seat over 75 people, and the building has many lobbies, rooms and an incredible backstage area.

The Roxy fell on some hard times, and is currently under reconstruction, but that has not stopped creative artists from producing some wonderful theater and other entertainment productions. There are rumors the place is haunted, but that just adds to the intrigue of this gorgeous location!

If you want to see some wonderful theater here, check this page often for schedule productions, listed below. You can also visit the Roxy in-world and look at the signs and marquee for all current productions.


CURRENT PRODUCTIONS AT THE ROXY!

Mint Entertainment is planning to launch the new Roxy Theater with their first theatrical production - "Pygmalion," written by George Benard Shaw.

Mint Entertainment will be holding auditions and casting this show on a soon to be announced date. Contact Zoltan Mint if you are interested in being involved in this production.

Pygmalion is a 1912 play by George Bernard Shaw, named after a Greek mythological character of the same name.

Professor of phonetics Henry Higgins makes a bet that he can train a bedraggled Cockney flower girl, Eliza Doolittle, to pass for a duchess at an ambassador's garden party by teaching her to assume a veneer of gentility, the most important element of which, he believes, is impeccable speech. The play is a sharp lampoon of the rigid British class system of the day and a commentary on women's independence
