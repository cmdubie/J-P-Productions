Encounter Boxes

What are Encounter Boxes?

Encounter Boxes were made for the discriminate role player on Second Life. They allow you to play out encounters with your friends, using minimum prims and space. All you need to do is select the genre and scene from your game, and rez the box in the sky at any height you wish.

Each Encounter Box was designed for minimal land impact, yet contain all the essential scenery for role playing a realistic and "cinematic" encounter. Because they are copyable, you can rez as many as your sim allows. You no longer have to spend countless hours building encounter areas for your role playing sessions!

You no longer have to worry about prim allocations or searching your inventory endlessly to create a scene that may only be used for a few minutes. With Encounter Boxes, you just rez the box, teleport inside, and play! Use Encounter Boxes for long communication sessions with players and other key characters in the story lines you are playing out. Use the boxes for combat sessions, or encounters where there may require realistic surroundings. The Boxes can be used for specific trap locations or treasure finds. There is literally no end to the uses of these ultra cool role playing aids.Use these boxes for what ever you wish!

They are not just limited for role playing purposes. Many of the homes and apartments can be used as real homes - completely furnished and ready to be lived in. Attention to detail and realism was our goal and, although these boxes are not modifiable, you can add what ever you wish to any of the constructs.

They make wonderful "movie sets" for all your machinima projects! Minty Entertainment has and will create hundreds of these Encounter Boxes, spanning many genres, including; Medieval, Fantasy, Sci-Fi, Old West, Steampunk, Gothic, Modern, Cyberpunk, Victorian, Near Future, and tons more. An in-world store is planned soon! See below for the listings of our current boxes.

Instructions For Use

To rez your Encounter Box, first drag the selected box from your inventory on the ground at a fairly clear location, if possible. Then have your avatar "sit" on the edge of the box at any location on the outside. Right click the box and select "Edit" from the menu. While in this edit mode, click the "Object" tab. Once there, find the "Position" area, specifically the blue "Z" coordinates. That is the current height of your Encounter Box in meters. Carefully change that number to the desired height you wish your box to be in the sky. (We recommend anything higher than 500 meters, for then people on the ground will not see the box.) After you change this coordinate, hit enter and you and your box will be whisked up to the new location. (In some viewers, you may have to renew your view of your camera.)Stand up and select "Fly" right away, so not to fall to the ground. Land on the top of your Encounter Box and enter the box. We recommend camming into the box and double click teleporting to the selected spot. (To activate Double Click Teleport, go to Avatar - Preferences - Move and View Tab - Movement tab. At the "Double Click on Land" option, select "Teleport to clicked point.")Once inside, find a good, neutral spot and immediately create a Landmark. (World - "Landmark This Place.") Now you can return to the box when ever you wish by using the Landmark in your inventory. You can also give your players and friends this link.

Keep in mind that the Encounter Boxes are a mix of items and scripts that may be modifiable and copyable. (Most of the prims in the Boxes are.) But some items may not be because of reasons beyond our control. Feel free to change the scenes as you wish, adding your own content as needed. You could even rez several Boxes and link them for a larger or longer encounter area, though some skill in Second Life building may be required. If you have any technical issues with your Encounter Box, please leave Zoltan Mint a message in the form of a note card. He will get back with you with in 24 hours.

Have fun with your Encounter Boxes, and please don't forget to leave a positive review on Marketplace!
